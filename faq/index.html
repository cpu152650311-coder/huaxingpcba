Ordering & Pricing

PCB Capabilities

We manufacture up to 32 layers for rigid PCBs. Our high-layer-count capability includes blind, buried, and back-drill vias, with precision impedance control (±5%). For HDI designs, we support any-layer microvia technology with laser-drilled vias down to 0.075mm.

We stock FR-4 (standard, high-Tg, halogen-free), Rogers (4350B, 4003C, 3003), Taconic (RF-35, TLX), Isola (370HR, FR408HR), Panasonic Megtron 6, Polyimide (flex/rigid-flex), Aluminum (MCPCB), Copper-core, Al₂O₃ and AlN ceramic. For materials not in stock, procurement lead time is typically 3–7 days. We can source virtually any laminate — just ask.

We offer: ENIG (electroless nickel immersion gold), ENEPIG, HASL lead-free, OSP, Immersion Silver, Immersion Tin, and Hard Gold (for edge connectors). ENIG is our most popular finish — excellent solderability, flat surface for fine-pitch components, and 12+ month shelf life. See our PCB fabrication page for a full finish comparison.

Our standard capability is 3/3 mil trace/space with 0.15mm minimum mechanical drill. For advanced designs, we achieve 2.5/2.5 mil and 0.10mm laser drill. We recommend staying at 5/5 mil or above for cost-optimized production. All trace/space values are verified during DFM review against your target IPC class.

Yes, impedance control to ±5% tolerance is a standard offering. We use Polar Instruments Si8000 for stackup simulation before production. Common targets: 50Ω single-ended, 90Ω / 100Ω differential. We provide impedance test coupons with every controlled-impedance order and include the test report in your QC documentation package.

Yes. We manufacture flex (1–8 layers) and rigid-flex (2–20 layers) with polyimide base material. Capabilities include: laser-cut coverlay openings, stiffeners (FR-4, polyimide, stainless steel), PSA adhesives, and ZIF connector-compatible thickness. Common applications: wearables, aerospace, medical devices, foldable consumer electronics.

PCBA Assembly

We operate 8 high-speed SMT lines with a combined capacity of 8 million placements per day. Each line features: automatic solder paste printer, 3D SPI (solder paste inspection), high-speed chip shooters + multi-function placers, 10-zone reflow ovens, and inline AOI. Component range: 0201 passives to 55×55mm BGAs, 0.3mm pitch QFNs.

We source from 50+ franchised distributors including Digi-Key, Mouser, Arrow, Avnet, Future Electronics, and LCSC. All components are traceable to manufacturer COO. We can also work with your consigned parts. For EOL or hard-to-find components, our procurement team performs cross-reference verification and authenticity testing before use.

Every PCBA order undergoes: 3D SPI (before reflow) → AOI (after reflow, both sides) → X-Ray (BGA/QFN joints) → ICT or flying probe (electrical verification) → functional test (per your specification). First-article inspection (FAI) is performed on every new design. We comply with IPC-A-610 Class 2 and Class 3 acceptance criteria.

Yes. We apply acrylic, silicone, and polyurethane conformal coatings via selective robotic spray or manual brush for low volumes. Coating thickness is verified per IPC-CC-830. We also offer potting/encapsulation for harsh-environment electronics. Common applications: automotive under-hood, outdoor industrial, marine electronics.

Turnkey: we procure all components, manage inventory, and handle assembly — you provide BOM and Gerber, we deliver assembled boards. Consigned: you ship us the components, we assemble and test. Turnkey saves you procurement time and leverages our distributor relationships for better pricing. Consigned works well when you have existing component inventory or proprietary parts.

Quality & Certifications

We are certified to: ISO 9001:2015 (quality management), ISO 14001:2015 (environmental management), IATF 16949 (automotive quality), and UL (safety). We are RoHS and REACH compliant. Our facility is audited quarterly by internal teams and annually by TÜV for ISO/IATF surveillance. Certificates available upon request.

IPC Class 2 (Dedicated Service): suitable for commercial/industrial products where extended life and uninterrupted service are desired. Allows minor visual imperfections. IPC Class 3 (High Reliability): required for automotive, medical, aerospace, and military — zero-defect tolerance. Tightens annular ring, solder joint, and plating requirements. Class 3 adds approximately 20–30% to PCB cost due to tighter process controls and 100% inspection.

We use a statistical process control (SPC) system that monitors key parameters in real time: plating thickness, etch factor, line width, registration accuracy. Each batch includes microsection coupons for cross-section analysis. We retain production records, test data, and retained samples for 3 years per IATF requirements. Re-orders use your approved panel layout and process parameters — no deviation without your approval.

Every order includes: 100% AOI (automated optical inspection), 100% electrical test (flying probe or fixture, per IPC-9252), visual final inspection, dimensional verification, and solderability testing on coupons. Impedance test reports are included for controlled-impedance designs. Microsection analysis is performed on a per-batch sampling basis. All test data is included in your QC documentation package.

If any board fails our outgoing QC inspection, we perform a root cause analysis and either rework or remanufacture the affected boards — at our cost. For critical failures, the entire batch is remanufactured. Our first-pass yield is 98.7% across all product types. For IPC Class 3 orders, any non-conformance triggers a full batch review and corrective action report (CAR).

Technical & File Requirements

We accept Gerber RS-274X (.gbr, .gerber) and Excellon NC Drill (.drl, .txt) as the standard fabrication package. For PCBA, we also need BOM (.csv, .xlsx, .xls) and Pick & Place / CPL (.csv). We can work with ODB++ and IPC-2581 if available. Our CAM team handles conversion from all major EDA tools: Altium, Eagle, KiCad, OrCAD, Allegro, EasyEDA, Diptrace, PADS, and Proteus.

A complete BOM should include: designator (reference ID), MPN (manufacturer part number), quantity, description/package type, and preferred manufacturer. Optional but helpful: alternate MPNs (for sourcing flexibility), Digi-Key/Mouser part numbers, and any special notes (e.g., "do not substitute", "matched pair"). Our procurement team reviews the BOM and flags any availability issues before we quote.

Design for Manufacturability (DFM) review is our engineering team's pre-production analysis of your design files to identify potential manufacturing issues. We check: trace/space violations, annular ring adequacy, solder mask slivers, acid traps, copper-to-edge clearance, drill aspect ratios, and impedance stackup feasibility. A good DFM review catches issues that would cause fabrication defects — saving you time, cost, and re-spins. Our DFM review is free and included with every quote.

Yes. Our engineering team provides design guidance for new clients: we'll review your concept, recommend stackup configurations, suggest cost-optimized material choices, and advise on DFM best practices. We don't offer full PCB layout services, but we can connect you with trusted design partners. For startups and first-time PCB buyers, we recommend starting with a DFM tips review and sending us your files early — even rough drafts — for preliminary feedback.

Gerber files (RS-274X) are the industry-standard format for PCB fabrication data — one file per layer (copper, solder mask, silkscreen, board outline). Every EDA tool has a CAM processor or "Plot" function to export Gerbers. Typically: File → Plot / Fabrication Outputs → select layers → check "RS-274X" format → generate. We have a detailed guide in our Gerber files guide. If you're stuck, send us your EDA source files and we'll help with the export.
