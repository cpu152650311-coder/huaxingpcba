Femtosecond laser-cut stencils with smooth aperture walls for consistent paste release. 304 stainless steel, ±5μm precision, nano-coating, same-day turnaround available.

The Technology

Why Laser-Cut Matters

Femtosecond Laser

Ultra-short pulse duration eliminates heat-affected zone. No recast layer on aperture walls — paste releases cleanly, aperture after aperture. Smooth sidewalls with Ra < 1.0μm without secondary electropolish for standard applications.

±5μm Positional Accuracy

Linear motor stages with laser interferometer feedback deliver ±5μm positional accuracy across the entire stencil area. Aperture-to-pad registration verified against your Gerber data before the stencil leaves our facility.

Trapezoidal Aperture Profile

Laser-cut apertures have a natural trapezoidal cross-section — wider on the squeegee side, narrower on the board-contact side. This geometry improves paste release by reducing stiction as the board separates from the stencil after the print stroke.

Material & Thickness Options

Standard material is 304 stainless steel (1.4301), selected for its balance of hardness, spring properties, and corrosion resistance. Foil thickness ranges from 0.08mm to 0.20mm in 0.02mm increments. Thinner foils (0.08–0.10mm) are used for fine-pitch applications where paste volume must be tightly controlled; thicker foils (0.15–0.20mm) suit power electronics and through-hole solder paste applications requiring higher deposit volumes.

Nickel electroformed stencils are available as a premium option for ultra-fine-pitch applications (0.3mm pitch QFN, 0201 metric). Nickel offers superior hardness and smoother aperture walls than stainless steel, extending stencil life to 50,000+ print cycles with the nano-coating option.

Nano-Coating for Fine-Pitch

Our proprietary nano-coating applies a sub-micron fluoropolymer layer to the stencil surface and aperture walls. The coating reduces solder paste adhesion to the stencil by 60–80%, enabling consistent paste release from apertures as small as 0.15 × 0.15mm without bridging between adjacent pads.

Nano-coating is strongly recommended for designs with 0201 passives, 0.3–0.4mm pitch QFNs/BGAs, or any board where paste release consistency directly impacts first-pass yield. Coated stencils last 3–5× longer than uncoated — fewer cleaning cycles, less aperture wear from squeegee abrasion.

Framed vs Frameless

Framed Stencil

  • 304 SS foil mesh-mounted on precision aluminum frame
  • Mesh tension verified at 35–45 N/cm² before shipping
  • Standard frame size: 736×736mm (29")
  • Custom frame sizes supported for non-standard printers
  • Ready to mount on your printer — no tensioning system needed
  • Nano-coating recommended: extends life 3–5×, improves release
  • Ideal for production volumes and repeat orders

Frameless (Foil-Only)

  • Laser-cut foil without frame — fits universal tensioning frames
  • 0.08–0.15mm thickness options (can go thinner than framed)
  • Standard sizes: 584×584mm, 650×650mm, 736×736mm
  • Compatible with VectorGuard, QuattroFlex, DEK, MPM systems
  • Lower cost per stencil — ideal for prototyping and low volume
  • Ships flat in rigid protective packaging
  • Compact storage: multiple foils in a single drawer
