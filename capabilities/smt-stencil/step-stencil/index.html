Step Stencil — Multi-Level Paste Deposition

Different paste volumes for different components — on the same board, in a single print stroke. Solve the fine-pitch vs. power-component paste volume conflict with precision-engineered step stencils.

The Problem Step Stencils Solve

Standard stencils have uniform thickness across the entire foil. For a board carrying only 0.5mm-pitch QFNs, a 0.10mm stencil delivers ideal paste volume. But add large power MOSFETs or through-hole connectors requiring intrusive reflow solder paste on the same board, and that 0.10mm thickness starves them of solder — leading to insufficient fillets, weak joints, and field failures.

A thicker stencil (0.15mm+) solves the power-component problem but deposits too much paste on fine-pitch pads — causing bridging, solder balls, and shorts on QFNs and 0201 passives. Step stencils eliminate this trade-off by manufacturing different thickness zones in a single stencil foil, each optimized for the component mix in that area.

Step-Up vs Step-Down Designs

Step-Down Stencil

The most common configuration. The stencil starts at a thicker base (e.g., 0.15mm) and is locally etched thinner (e.g., 0.10mm) in the fine-pitch zones on the squeegee side.

  • Pocket etched into squeegee side — downward step
  • Ideal when most of the board needs thick paste
  • Fine-pitch areas get reduced volume
  • Step transition needs ≥ 2mm clearance from nearest aperture
  • Common for motor drives, power supplies, LED driver boards

Step-Up Stencil

The reverse approach. A thinner base stencil (e.g., 0.10mm) has material added locally (e.g., 0.18mm) for power-component zones on the board-contact side. More complex to manufacture but protects fine-pitch print quality.

  • Material added on board-contact side — upward step
  • Base preserves excellent fine-pitch release
  • Thick zones supply extra paste to power pads
  • Requires ≥ 3mm clearance from step edge to aperture
  • Preferred for densely populated IoT/telecom boards

Where Step Stencils Excel

Motor Controllers & Drives

Boards with DSP/MCU fine-pitch logic alongside large IGBT modules, MOSFET arrays, and high-current bus bar pads. Step-down stencil delivers 0.10mm on the logic side, 0.18mm on power devices — in one print stroke.

Automotive ECUs

Modern ECUs pack 0201 decoupling caps, 0.4mm-pitch BGAs, and large connector footprints on one board. A well-designed step stencil prevents bridging on the processor while ensuring sufficient paste for high-reliability connector solder joints.

IoT & Wireless Modules

Compact wireless modules with fine-pitch RF shields and chip antennas alongside power-management ICs with exposed thermal pads. Step stencil lets you optimize paste for both without compromising RF grounding or thermal performance.

Design Guidelines for Step Stencils

Effective step stencil design requires planning at the PCB layout stage. Keep-out zones of 2–3mm around step transitions ensure the squeegee blade rides smoothly and the stencil gaskets properly against the board surface. We recommend grouping fine-pitch components together in one contiguous zone and power/high-volume paste components in another.

The step transition itself is a gradual ramp (approximately 1mm per 0.02mm step height), not an abrupt wall. Our CAM engineers review your step plan during the DFM stage and may recommend zone adjustments to avoid aperture-distortion risk near transition areas. Multi-level stencils (3+ thickness levels) are possible but add complexity; we typically recommend a maximum of two step levels for reliable print performance.

Step Stencil Capabilities

Step Configuration

Step-down (pocket on squeegee side) and step-up (raised on board-contact side). Two-level standard, three-level on request. Step height from 0.02mm to 0.10mm differential.

Maximum Thickness Range

Base foil 0.08–0.15mm. Stepped zones 0.10–0.20mm. Total differential up to 0.10mm. Combination example: 0.10mm base + 0.08mm step-down = 0.18mm / 0.10mm zones.

Clearance Requirements

Step-down: ≥ 2mm from step edge to nearest aperture. Step-up: ≥ 3mm clearance. Transition ramp: approximately 1mm per 0.02mm step. Detailed zone map reviewed during DFM.

Printer Compatibility

Compatible with all major automatic printers (DEK Horizon/Eclipse, MPM Momentum/Accela, Ekra, GKG, Desen). Step stencils are framed only — frameless step stencils are not recommended.

Foil Material

304 stainless steel foil, mesh-mounted on precision aluminum frame. Nano-coating available on step stencils — recommended for fine-pitch zones to maintain release performance.
