Why Impedance Matters

Signal Integrity Starts with the Stackup

At frequencies above 50 MHz, PCB traces behave as transmission lines. Mismatched impedance causes signal reflections, ringing, crosstalk, and bit errors. Controlled-impedance PCB design ensures that every high-speed signal sees a consistent characteristic impedance along its entire path — from driver to receiver. Our engineering team calculates trace width, spacing, and dielectric thickness for each controlled-impedance layer, then verifies every panel with TDR measurement.

Single-Ended vs Differential Pairs

Single-Ended

50Ω

One signal trace referenced to a ground plane. Most common impedance target for RF and general high-speed traces.

  • Microstrip: trace on outer layer over reference plane
  • Stripline: trace sandwiched between two reference planes
  • Co-planar waveguide: trace with adjacent ground pour
  • Typical Dk range: 3.2-4.8 (determines trace width)

Differential Pair

90Ω / 100Ω

Two coupled traces carrying complementary signals. Superior noise immunity and common-mode rejection for high-speed data.

  • Edge-coupled: two traces side-by-side on same layer
  • Broadside-coupled: traces on adjacent layers (less common)
  • 90Ω: USB 2.0/3.0, HDMI, DisplayPort
  • 100Ω: Ethernet (1000BASE-T, 10GBASE-T), PCIe all generations

TDR Verification

Every Panel Measured & Verified

TDR (Time Domain Reflectometry) sends a fast-rise-time pulse down a test coupon trace and measures reflections. Any impedance variation shows as a deviation in the reflected waveform. We place impedance test coupons on every controlled-impedance panel, test each coupon, and provide a detailed TDR report with your shipment. Typical coupon length: 150mm (6 inches) with SMA or probe-pad launch structures.

  • Tektronix DSA8300 sampling oscilloscope with 80E04 TDR module
  • Rise time: 35ps (enables resolution of sub-mm discontinuities)
  • Test report: impedance vs. distance plot for each controlled trace
  • Correlation coupons match your actual production panel construction
