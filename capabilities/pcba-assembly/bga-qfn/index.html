Void rate consistently under 5% per IPC-7095 Class 3 criteria — verified on every BGA by X-ray. Achieved through optimized ramp-soak-spike profiles, nitrogen atmosphere, and vacuum soldering option for ultra-low void applications. Solder paste selection tuned per alloy and ball metallurgy.

QFN

Sidewall Wetting

QFN and DFN packages present unique challenges with the exposed pad and invisible sidewall fillets. We optimize stencil aperture design (window-pane pattern for the thermal pad), paste volume, and reflow profile to achieve IPC-compliant sidewall wetting. X-ray verification confirms pad coverage and void level.

Dedicated BGA rework station with precision bottom-side preheating and top-side focused IR/convection. 3D X-Ray / CT

Computed tomography X-ray for complex BGA and stacked-die packages. 3D reconstruction reveals void distribution through the ball height, partial non-wets that 2D X-ray misses, and head-in-pillow defects. Used for NPI qualification and production auditing on Class 3 builds.

Verification

X-Ray Inspection — Every BGA, Every Board

Unlike peripheral-leaded components where solder joints are visible to AOI, BGA and QFN joints are hidden under the package body. X-ray inspection is the only non-destructive method to verify solder joint quality. We X-ray 100% of BGA and QFN sites on every production board — not just a sample.

  • 2D X-ray — Real-time transmission imaging. Automated void calculation with pass/fail thresholds per IPC-7095. Quick scan for bridging, insufficient solder, missing balls, and gross voiding. Programmable inspection paths per board design.
  • 3D CT X-ray — Computed tomography reconstructs a full 3D model of every ball. Reveals void distribution through the ball height (planar vs. spherical voids), partial non-wets, and head-in-pillow defects invisible in 2D. Used for NPI and Class 3 verification.
  • Void analysis software — Automatic measurement of void area percentage per ball. SPC tracking across production lots. Any ball exceeding IPC-7095 void criteria is flagged for engineering review.

Defect Prevention

Common BGA/QFN Defects — and How We Prevent Them

BGA and QFN assembly defects are predictable when you understand the root causes. Our process controls address each failure mode before it reaches production — from stencil design through reflow profile to X-ray verification.

  • Head-in-Pillow (HiP) — The ball wets to the paste but the paste never wets to the pad. Caused by excessive oxidation or insufficient soak time. Prevented by nitrogen atmosphere reflow, controlled time-above-liquidus, and fresh solder paste with verified shelf life.
  • Non-Wet Open (NWO) — Complete failure of the ball to wet to the pad. Often caused by warpage lifting the package during reflow. Mitigated by profiling actual component temperature (thermocouple on ball), optimized ramp rate, and board support tooling.
  • Excessive Voiding — Outgassing flux volatiles trapped in the molten solder. Reduced through optimized paste chemistry, extended soak zone for volatile escape, and vacuum soldering option for critical applications.

QFN-Specific Process Controls

⚙

Thermal Pad Void Control

The exposed thermal pad under a QFN is prone to voiding because flux volatiles have limited escape paths. We use window-pane stencil aperture patterns (30-50% coverage, segmented) with via-in-pad designs for outgassing. Vacuum reflow available for <10% void rate on thermal pads in automotive and medical applications.

🔍

Sidewall Fillet Inspection

IPC-A-610 requires a visible solder fillet on at least one side of a QFN lead (Class 2) or on the toe and both sides (Class 3). We verify sidewall wetting through a combination of high-angle AOI cameras and X-ray. Accept/reject criteria are programmed per IPC class for each package type on the board.

📏

Stencil Design for QFN

QFN pad geometry demands precise stencil aperture design. Perimeter pads require 1:1 aperture-to-pad ratio with rounded corners for paste release. Thermal pad requires segmented apertures for void reduction. Stepped stencils (thinner for fine-pitch QFN, thicker for power components) on the same board.

🌡

Profile Optimization

QFN packages have lower thermal mass than BGAs but the hidden joints mean you can't visually confirm reflow. We attach thermocouples directly to QFN leads and thermal pads during profile development. The reflow profile is tuned so the QFN sidewall reaches full liquidus temperature without overheating nearby small-chip components.
