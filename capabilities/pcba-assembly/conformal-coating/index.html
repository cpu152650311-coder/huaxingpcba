Coating Chemistries

Four Materials. One Standard.

AR — Acrylic

General Purpose

Solvent-based acrylic is the most widely used conformal coating. Good moisture and fungus resistance. Easy to apply and easy to rework — soldering iron burns through it for component replacement. Operating range -65C to +125C. Ideal for consumer electronics, IoT devices, and general industrial controls where moderate protection is sufficient and reworkability matters.

SR — Silicone

High Temperature

Silicone coatings operate from -65C to +200C, making them the only choice for under-hood automotive electronics, downhole drilling equipment, and aerospace engine controls. Excellent flexibility absorbs thermal expansion mismatch. Higher moisture permeability than acrylic but far superior in thermal cycling endurance. Available in UV-cure formulations for fast processing.

UR — Polyurethane

Chemical Resistance

Polyurethane provides the best chemical and abrasion resistance of the liquid coatings. Resists fuels, solvents, hydraulic fluids, and cleaning agents. Common in fuel system electronics, industrial process controls, and marine equipment. Single-component moisture-cure formulations eliminate mixing errors. Harder to rework than acrylic — plan for no post-coat servicing.

XY — Parylene

Nano-Thin Protection

Parylene is applied by vapor deposition in a vacuum chamber — not as a liquid. It forms a truly conformal, pinhole-free film as thin as 0.1 microns. Penetrates under components and into crevices impossible to reach with spray. The gold standard for medical implants, military avionics, and MEMS devices. Highest unit cost but unmatched protection-per-micron.

Application Methods

Spray, Dip, Brush — and Selective

Coating application method is as critical as coating chemistry. We select the method based on board geometry, keep-out zones, production volume, and coating thickness requirements. Most production boards use our Nordson Asymtek Select Coat SC-350 for automated selective application — programmable per-board, precise to 0.5mm keep-out accuracy.

  • Selective spray (automated) — Programmable X-Y robot with micro-spray valve. Applies coating only where specified. No masking needed for connectors, test points, or heatsinks. Thickness controlled by flow rate, traverse speed, and number of passes. Ideal for medium-to-high volume with complex keep-out requirements.
  • Dip coating — Board immersed in coating bath then withdrawn at controlled speed. Provides 100% coverage including under components. Used when complete encapsulation is required and connectors are not yet installed. Most uniform thickness of all methods. Best for high-voltage isolation.
  • Manual spray / brush — For prototypes, low volume, and touch-up after automated coating. Experienced operators apply coating per IPC-CC-830 workmanship standards. UV tracer dye in coating material enables immediate inspection.

Quality Assurance

UV Inspection & Thickness Verification

Every coated board passes through UV light inspection. All coating materials contain a UV tracer dye that fluoresces under 365nm UV light — making coverage gaps, thin spots, and overspray immediately visible. We verify coating thickness with an eddy-current probe on test coupons processed alongside every batch.

  • UV fluorescence inspection — 100% visual inspection under UV-A light. Any uncoated area appears dark against the bright fluorescent coating. Operators check coverage on all edges, corners, tall components, and keep-out zone boundaries. IPC-CC-830 requires complete coverage on specified areas with no bubbles, cracks, or delamination.
  • Thickness measurement — Coating thickness measured per IPC-CC-830 Table 3-1 requirements (typically 30-130 microns for acrylic/urethane, 50-210 for silicone). Eddy-current probe on test coupons. Data recorded per batch for traceability.
  • Adhesion testing (on request) — Cross-hatch tape test per ASTM D3359 for coating adhesion verification. Required for certain automotive and military specifications.

Application Methods by Use Case

01

Automotive Under-Hood

Silicone coating, selective spray. Withstands -40C to +150C cycling, transmission fluid exposure, and vibration. Typical thickness: 50-100 microns. Connectors and OBD port masked during coating. Common for ECU, TCU, ABS controllers, and battery management systems.

02

Marine & Outdoor

Polyurethane coating, full dip or spray. Salt spray resistance per ASTM B117. UV-stable formulations prevent coating degradation in sunlight. Typical thickness: 75-130 microns. Used on navigation, communication, and deck equipment where salt water exposure is continuous.

03

Medical Implantable

Parylene Type C, vapor deposited. Biocompatible per ISO 10993. Ultrathin (5-25 microns), pinhole-free, conforms to sub-micron topography. Applied in cleanroom conditions. Used for neural stimulators, cochlear implants, and cardiac rhythm management devices.

04

Consumer / IoT

Acrylic coating, selective spray or brush. Basic moisture and dust protection at the lowest cost. Easy rework for prototype and development boards. Typical thickness: 30-50 microns. Provides the minimum environmental barrier needed for indoor consumer products.

Potting & Encapsulation

When a thin coating is not enough — full encapsulation embeds the entire PCB assembly in a solid block of protective material. Complete waterproofing, shock resistance, and tamper-proofing for the most demanding environments.

EP

Epoxy Potting

Two-part epoxy provides the highest mechanical strength and chemical resistance. Hard, rigid, non-reworkable. Excellent thermal conductivity for heat-dissipating applications (LED drivers, power supplies). Operating range -55C to +150C. Vacuum degassing removes air bubbles before cure.

SI

Silicone Gel Encapsulation

Soft, flexible silicone gel for shock absorption and vibration damping. Transparent formulations allow visual inspection of components. Repairable — gel can be cut away for rework. Operating range -65C to +200C. Common in high-voltage power supplies and automotive sensors.
