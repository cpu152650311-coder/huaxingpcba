Process Step 1

Solder Paste Printing & SPI

Every SMT assembly starts with precision solder paste deposition. We use laser-cut stainless steel stencils (0.08–0.20mm thickness) on DEK Horizon 03iX printers with automatic optical alignment. The printer achieves ±12.5µm alignment at 2 Cpk — critical for fine-pitch pads.

  • Laser-cut stencils — electro-polished aperture walls for consistent paste release. Stepped and nano-coated stencils available for mixed-technology boards.
  • 100% SPI inspection — 3D solder paste inspection on every board. Volumetric measurement per pad. Catches insufficient paste, bridging, and smear before a single component is placed.
  • Automatic stencil cleaning — dry-wipe-vacuum cycle every 5-10 prints. Prevents aperture clogging that causes gradual paste volume drift over a production run.

Process Step 2-3

Placement & Reflow Profiling

After paste deposition and SPI verification, boards enter the placement and reflow sequence. High-speed placers (Yamaha YSM40R, 200K CPH) handle chip components while fine-pitch placers (Yamaha YSM20WR) handle BGAs and QFNs in the same pass. The entire line is synchronized for continuous flow.

  • 10-zone reflow — Heller 1913 Mk5 ovens with independent top/bottom zone control. Ramp-soak-spike profile tailored to your board's thermal mass. ±1.5°C control per zone.
  • Nitrogen atmosphere — O₂ maintained below 500 ppm throughout reflow. Eliminates oxidation during liquidus phase, producing brighter, stronger solder joints with fewer voids.
  • Profile development — Every new design gets a custom reflow profile. We attach thermocouples to critical components (largest BGA, smallest QFN, board edge) and verify ΔT stays within solder paste specifications.
